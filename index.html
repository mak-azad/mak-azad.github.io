About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer and Information Science at the University of Michigan–Dearborn, working in the SRLab on NSF-funded research. I combine program analysis with AI/machine learning — including large language models — to automatically detect and repair software performance bugs in HPC, cloud, and IoT systems. I built PcMINER, a knowledge-distilled transformer pipeline that mines performance-related commits at scale and was deployed on a 45-node CPU cluster to curate a large-scale performance-bug dataset.

Previously, I taught Computer Science as a Lecturer at Begum Rokeya University (Bangladesh) for seven years, coached ACM ICPC programming teams, and collaborated on big-data healthcare informatics research with The City University of Hong Kong.

Research Interests

  • Software Engineering & Systems: performance bugs, program analysis, mining software repositories, empirical software engineering
  • AI for Code: LLMs for code understanding, knowledge distillation, prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning
  • Applied ML / NLP: transformer models, generative AI, large-scale data curation
